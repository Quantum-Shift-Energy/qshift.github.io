Quantum Shift operates on a few explicit analytical commitments.

## Evidence first

Claims are grounded in data — publicly available datasets, regulatory filings, market data, engineering specifications. Where a claim rests on modelling assumptions, those assumptions are stated.

## Show the limits

Every model simplifies. Analysis here tries to be explicit about what is and isn't captured — what the numbers can tell you and where you need to reason differently.

## Operational grounding

Infrastructure analysis that ignores operational constraints is usually wrong. Grid capacity, dispatch logic, interconnection queues, permitting timelines — these shape outcomes as much as capital flows or policy intent.

## No house view

There is no political or commercial position to defend here. Conclusions follow from the analysis, not from a prior commitment to a particular outcome.
